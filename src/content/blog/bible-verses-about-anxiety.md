## What Jesus said about worry

> *"Therefore I say unto you, Take no thought for your life, what ye shall eat, or what ye shall drink; nor yet for your body, what ye shall put on. Is not the life more than meat, and the body than raiment?"* — [Matthew 6:25 KJV](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Matthew%206%3A25&version=KJV)

> *"Which of you by taking thought can add one cubit unto his stature?"* — [Matthew 6:27 KJV](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Matthew%206%3A27&version=KJV)

> *"But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you."* — [Matthew 6:33 KJV](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Matthew%206%3A33&version=KJV)

> *"Take therefore no thought for the morrow: for the morrow shall take thought for the things of itself. Sufficient unto the day is the evil thereof."* — [Matthew 6:34 KJV](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Matthew%206%3A34&version=KJV)

> *"Peace I leave with you, my peace I give unto you: not as the world giveth, give I unto you. Let not your heart be troubled, neither let it be afraid."* — [John 14:27 KJV](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=John%2014%3A27&version=KJV)

## A short word

The Bible's collection of verses on anxiety is large because anxiety is universal and God's response is consistent: *I am with thee*. The common thread across all these passages is not *the circumstance has changed* but *the Father is near*. The peace promised is His presence, not your changed weather.
